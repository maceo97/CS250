#How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

I would first talk to the user and see what they are looking for out of the program and what do they like and dislike about similar programs so that I can address their needs as efficiently as possible. I would then try to take the needs of the user coupled with what I think would make a program that is of value to the user, and implement my ideas from their via code. User stories help with this by giving the "who, what, and why" from users that descriptively show who will be using the program, what do they want from it, and why would this be beneficial.

#How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach programs by assessing what is being asked of me and how much effort will it take to complete each portion of the program. I then try to work through whatever seems to be the foundation of the program, then work my way up from there, testing out my program as much as I can in hopes of mitigating any issues arising later in the program. I hope to incorporate customer collaboration, iterative delivery, and flexibility in my future development work because throughout this course these principles seem to be the main factors of the Agile and I believe them to be integral in providing valuable programs to clients, customers, and teachers alike. 

#What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

I believe a good team member in software development is someone who stays on top of their work, is an effective communicator, and gives value to the team in all ways no matter what capacity they are involved. Being efficient and on time is important when being on a time because your work can directly affect others, communication is also of importance because that fosters the environment of collaboration and can lead to you getting help or helping others, and finally making sure to give value at all points of work in a team because redundant or unneccesary involvement in the team can just lead to confusion and clutter which doesn't support the team as a whole.
